What is Honeygain, And How Does It Work?

It is an app that allows users to earn money by sharing their unused internet connection.

Internet sharing can be either through Wi-Fi, mobile data, or both, and users can control the limit of sharing when it comes to mobile data.

Honeygain is basically a form of “automated passive income stream” as it generally involves no efforts on the users’ part.

We install it on our phones and computers devices and leave it running in the background. In short, we do not have to put in any effort at all. The amount of internet network we share gets converted into dollars and cents.

The unused internet connection we shared is gathered by the Honeygain network and used by researchers from e-commerce, advertising, and web intelligence companies. These companies extract insights from the web to enable topics of interest such as market research, brand protection and many others.

The company is a 100% remote company with a staff strength of over 20+ across different countries.

 

Generally speaking, HoneyGain is simply an “install and earn” mobile & computer app which is easy to use. All users need to do is allow the app to run in the background, to make some earnings.

What are the supported platforms?

For computer devices, it supports Windows, macOS and Linux operating systems.

For mobile devices, it currently only supports the Android operating system.

The reason why there is no iOS support is due to the operating system’s technical limitations.

Unlike Android, iOS does not allow apps to have “background service functionality”, which is essential for HoneyGain to operate in the background without having the user constantly open the app and interact with it to keep it active.

IOS users currently won’t use the HoneyGain app on their devices.

Content Delivery Explained

When you see the option to enable “Content Delivery” under your Settings, you should always do so. At the time of writing, it is available for most users in most countries.

Content Delivery allows users to share their internet connection for bandwidth-intensive content such as images, videos, audio, VoIP, etc.

As Content Delivery only works on Wi-Fi connections and computer devices, it is safe to enable and allow it to use in the background. Currently, you cannot enable Content Delivery on mobile devices.

Sharing a personal experience here. Sometime back, I had to restart my computer while having HoneyGain running for a long time. Upon resuming the HoneyGain app, I noticed that the content delivery mode went back to “In Queue” and remained this way for several days.

During the wait, I could not earn from Content Delivery, and it took several days before it resumed back to normal mode.

In short, there is no guarantee that you will get back to Content Delivery mode anytime soon if you happen to restart your device. Instead, it will go back to the queuing status, and you have to wait until it resumes.

Can I use VPN or Proxies?

I would strongly discourage the use of VPNs and Proxies.

The reason here lies in your I.P. address.

VPNs are often shared among many different users who connect themselves to that particular country server. Therefore, getting suspended when multiple HoneyGain accounts share the same I.P. address.

When you connect to a VPN and use HoneyGain, you may also encounter an error that says “Network overused”.

Disconnect your VPN connection and use your Wi-Fi or mobile data connection instead.

Are there any payout processing fees involved?

Choosing Paypal

When using the Paypal payout method, we need to remember that there will be two different processing fees involved.

One is fees imposed by Tipalti, and the other is fees charged by Paypal itself.

Using a real-life example that I had using the Paypal method, a sum of USD 1.47 is deducted from the actual payout request amount of USD 23.26, leaving the Net Amount to be USD 21.79.

Therefore USD 1.47 is the total transaction fees imposed by both Tipalti and Paypal, and this works out to approximately 6.32% of processing fees.

Fees by Tipalti are as follows;

ResidenceFees per payment
U.S. Resident USD $1 + 2% (up to USD $2 cap)
Non-US Resident USD $1 + 2% (up to USD $21 cap)

if you request a payout in a different currency than USD, an exchange fee of 2.50% applies.
